The Initiative for Promoting Digital Birth Registration In Bangsamoro Autonomous Region In Muslim Mindanao - Programming Services
Published on: 04 October 2024
Deadline: 18 October 2024 23:59 Philippines time
The Office of the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) was established on
December 14, 1950, by the United Nations General Assembly. The agency is mandated to lead and
co-ordinate international action to protect refugees and resolve refugee problems worldwide. Its
primary purpose is to safeguard the rights and well-being of refugees. It also has a mandate to help
stateless people.
To address these issues, UNHCR is initiating a project to support birth registration within the two
population groups along with decommissioned combatants and their families to facilitate access to
documentation to establish legal identity and contribute to sustainable solutions to end statelessness.
As part of this initiative, UNHCR will provide support to the digitization process of 50 local civil
registrars in BARMM with the objective of strengthening their intake and data management
capacities. This will be done through the provision of equipment and establishment of an archiving,
indexing, and recording system which reduces manual records processing. This will later allow ease
in integration with the civil registry digitalization efforts of the Philippine Statistics Authority
through its PhilCRRIS once it is fully rolled-out.
